About PTE Academic

PTE Academic is a fully computer-based English proficiency test designed to assess real-world communication skills using advanced AI-driven scoring. Trusted by thousands of institutions and governments worldwide, it evaluates your ability to understand and use English in academic and everyday contexts.

The test is divided into three main parts: Speaking & Writing (54–67 minutes), Reading (29–30 minutes), and Listening (30–43 minutes). All tasks are completed on a computer, and results are typically available within 48 hours.

AI-Powered Scoring: PTE uses automated scoring to ensure fairness, accuracy, and consistency, eliminating human bias and delivering reliable results for test-takers globally.

  • 3500+ institutions accept PTE Academic.
  • 48 hours average turnaround time for results.
  • 100% digital – no paper-based testing.
  • Recognised by immigration authorities in Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK.

Partners & Research

PTE HUB collaborates with leading academic institutions, research laboratories, and government bodies to advance the science of language assessment. Our partners help us refine AI scoring models, conduct validity studies, and ensure that PTE remains a fair and accurate measure of English proficiency.

Key research areas include acoustic modelling across accents, bias elimination in automated scoring, and concordance studies mapping PTE scores to other tests like IELTS and TOEFL. Our white papers and technical manuals are available for download, providing transparency into the test's development and validation.

Future Initiatives: We're exploring adaptive testing, LLM-assisted grammar diagnostics, and predictive scoring reports to make preparation even more personalised.

Universities Accepting PTE

PTE Academic is accepted by over 3,500 institutions worldwide, including top universities in Australia, Canada, the United Kingdom, New Zealand, and the United States. Whether you're applying for undergraduate, postgraduate, or doctoral programmes, PTE scores can help you meet English language requirements.

Typical score requirements vary by institution and course. Most universities ask for a minimum overall score between 50–65 for undergraduate programmes and 58–76 for postgraduate study. Some elite institutions like Oxford, Cambridge, and the University of Toronto may require higher scores (65+). Always check the specific requirements of your chosen university.

Quick filter: Our university directory lets you search by country and institution to find detailed admission information and PTE score expectations.

  • Australia: All Group of Eight universities accept PTE.
  • Canada: U15 research universities, including UBC and McGill.
  • UK: Russell Group universities, including Oxford and Cambridge.
  • New Zealand: All eight universities accept PTE Academic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to some of the most common questions about PTE Academic. For a complete list, visit our dedicated FAQ page.

  • What is the test duration? The total test time is approximately 2 hours.
  • How soon are results available? Typically within 48 hours of taking the test.
  • Is PTE easier than IELTS? It depends on your strengths; PTE is computer-based and uses AI scoring, which some find more predictable.
  • What is the scoring range? Scores range from 10 to 90, with increments of 1.
  • Can I retake the test? Yes, you can retake as many times as you wish, but you must wait for your results before rebooking.
  • How long are scores valid? PTE scores are valid for 2 years from the test date.
